origin

Stairs was originally created by cardboard for Ludum Dare 26. cardboard came up with the idea for stairs while away from his computer, surprisingly. Without access to his computer, he painstakingly pseudocoded his idea into his phone, so that he could code it quicker when he arrived home. By quicker, I mean in less than 10 hours, at least according to his blog post on the Ludum Dare website.

The game turned out how cardboard wanted it to, and he received positive and negative feedback. People called the game "funny", "very original", "QWOP-esque", and "about as minimalistic as it get".

about

Stairs is a 'infinite stepper' in the vein of Cube Runner or Super Hexagon.

The goal of stairs is to step up stairs as long as possible, without tripping, falling off, or skipping any steps.

A helpful hint: To get over a step, both feet cannot be on the ground at the moment a step leaves the screen. You can only lift one foot at a time.

There are two different game modes, each with four levels of difficulty. Levels has organized waves of stairs - zig zag, side to side, rapid fire, narrow, and random. Classic is where the stairs follow no patterns and are all random, and is based off of cardboard's original Ludum Dare submission. As you climb more steps, the game gets harder. The four difficulty levels are:

  • Easy - For beginners (e.g. Keegan). A stepping stone to the more advanced difficulties.
  • Medium - Moderately challenging. A respectable choice.
  • Hard - Hard is Medium, but at its hardest (remember how the game gets harder as you climb more stairs?), which is pretty darn hard. Unlock this difficulty by getting 150 on Medium.
  • Insane - If you score 100 on Insane I will buy you dinner. Because I made the game and have played it a lot and getting 100 on Insane is insane. Unlock this difficulty by getting 100 on Hard.
Note: Chances are, if you can't unlock a difficulty level, you probably won't fare too well with it.

The original Stairs was basically a prequel to the Classic game mode on easy difficulty. Whereas the motivation in the original Stairs was a motivational speech from a potato when one reached the top, the motivation in the new Stairs is to get higher scores than all your lame friends.

Matt and cardboard made this game. Matt programmed it and cardboard did the art and music. Special thanks to Kickstarter backers for helping us make this happen.

tutorial

hints

  • Stepping with a foot moves you in the opposite direction
    • Stepping with your left foot moves you right (or the stairs left)
    • Stepping with your right foot moves you left (or the stairs right)
  • Sometimes you will need to look ahead and step strategically to line yourself up for future steps
  • Smaller steps are usually more effective than bigger steps
    • This is especially true for narrow stairs
    • The main exception to this method is when you need to move horizontally
  • Don't die! The following are the ways to die:
    • Trip: This happens when the top of the closest stair leaves the screen while both of your feet are grounded
    • Fall off left: This happens when the top of the closest stair leaves the screen while your left foot is on the ground but off the stair
    • Fall off right: Same as the above, but with the right foot being the one that is off the stair
    • Step off left: This happens when the top of the closest stair leaves the screen and your left foot is airborne to the left of the stair
    • Step off right: Sane as the above, but with the right foot being airborne
    • Skip a step: This happens when you skip a step; take it one stair at a time!
    • Important takeaway: The thing that matters is the TOP of the steps. This is when the conditions for dieing are checked
